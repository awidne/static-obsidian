# Neurological Exam

## Background
A quick neurologic exam is crucial for ruling out ominous diagnoses. In the emergency department, this should not take longer than 5 minutes.
## Exam
### Mental status
Assessed during history taking. Cognition and speech should be noted in particular.
### Gait, truncal stability, and proprioception
Normal, heel, toe, and tandem gait should be assessed. Truncal stability can be assessed during gait assessment of when seated.

Pronator drift and Romberg test should be done as well.
### Cranial nerves
Assessment of CN II-XII:
- Pupil response (PERRLA = pupils equal, round, reactive to light, and accommodation) (CN II-III)
- Extraocular movements (CN III, IV, VI)
- Facial sensation and masseter function (CN V)
- Puff out cheeks, show teeth (CN VII)
- Hearing (assessed during history) (CN VIII)
- Symmetric palate elevation and tongue extension with "ah" (CN IX, X, XII)
- Turn head against resistance (CN XI)
### Motor
#### Upper motor
- Elbow flexion and extension
- Finger grip
- Finger abduction and adduction
#### Lower motor
- Hip flexion
- Knee extension
- Foot dorsiflexion and plantarflexion
- Great toe extension
### Sensory
Comparison of distal extremities for assymmetry.
### Coordination
Gross coordination: patient tracks their finger from clinician finger to patient nose
Fine coordination: rapid alternating dorsal/palmar hands
### Reflexes
Test as needed.
