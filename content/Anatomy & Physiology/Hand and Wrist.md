# Hand and Wrist
## Skeleton
Connected to the radius and ulna are the **carpal bones** of the wrist. These form two rows and are represented by the mnemonic "So Long To Pinky, Here Comes The Thumb"

Thumb side -- pinky side

| **Trapezium** | **Trapezoid** | **Capitate**    | **Hamate**   |
| ------------- | ------------- | --------------- | ------------ |
| **Scaphoid**  | **Lunate**    | **Triquestrum** | **Pisiform** |

Attached to the carpal bones are the five **metacarpal bones**. Each of which are attached the **phalanges**.

There are 14 total phalanges with three making up each finger (distal, middle, and proximal) and two making up the thumb.

## Innervation
Sensory innervation:
![[Hand and Wrist-1.png]]

**Radial Nerve**
Motor function:
- Wrist extension
	- Extensor carpi radialis longus/brevis
	- Extensor carpi ulnaris
- Finger and thumb extension
	- Posterior interosseous nerve (PIN) branch

Test function with wrist extension against resistance.
- Injury sign: wrist drop (inability to extend)
- Common injury site: spiral groove of humerus ("Saturday night palsy") or radial head fracture

**Median Nerve**
The median nerve enters the hand through the carpal tunnel. Motor function are:
- Flexors (forearm)
	- Flexor digitalis superficialis (PIP flexion for digit 2-5)
	- Flexor digitalis profundus (DIP flexion for digit 2-3) via anterior interosseous nerve
	- Flexor carpi radialis (flexion and radial deviation of wrist)
	- Pronator teres (forearm pronation, weak elbow flexion)
	- Pronator quadratus (forearm pronation)
- Thenar muscles (hand)
	- Abductor pollicis brevis (thumb abduction)
	- Opponens pollicis (thumb opposition; flexion, adduction, and medial rotation at CMC joint)
	- Flexor pollicis longus (flexes thumb IP joint) via AIN

Test function with "OK" sign, make fist
- Injury sign: loss of thumb opposition and thenar wasting, ape hand (extension of digit 1-3 when trying to make a fist)
- Common injury site: carpal tunnel, supracondylar fracture

**Ulnar Nerve**
Motor function are:
- Flexion
	- Flexor carpi ulnaris (wrist flexion, ulnar deviation)
	- Flexor digitalis profundus (DIP flexion for digit 4-5)
	- Lumbricals (MCP flexion, IP extension of digit 4-5)
	- Flexor pollicis brevis (thumb MCP flexion)
- Abduction
	- Hypothenar muscles (abduction, opposition, flexion of digit 5)
	- Dorsal interossei (finger abduction)
- Adduction
	- Palmar interossei (finger adduction)
	- Adductor pollicis (thumb adduction)

Test function with observation at rest.
- Injury sign: claw hand (hyperextension of digit & flexion of digit 4-5 at rest), loss of abduction/adduction
- Common injury site: medial epicondyle (cubital tunnel) or hook of hamate
